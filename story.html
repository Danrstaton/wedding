Dan & Clay

Our Story

The beginning

How they met

Dan and Clay's story begins shortly after Dan relocated from Boston to San Diego. In August 2021, they crossed paths during a day at the beach. A few weeks passed and the two ultimately reconnected online, meeting for their first date at the Trolley Barn Park in University Heights. What was meant to be an afternoon picnic turned into hours of conversation, concluding with dinner and drinks nearby.

Conveniently, Clay and Dan lived only a few blocks away from each other in North Park and began a ritual of evening walks and weekends adventuring around town. As the two became more enamored with each other, they made things official that December and have been together since.

Adventures along the way

Growing together

Clay and Dan's lives became increasingly intertwined, spending weekends and holidays with each other's friends and family on both sides of the country. Later that year, the pair moved in together near the location of their first date. Beyond their happiness at home, they continued to indulge in their love of travel. From beer halls in Munich to onsens in Kyoto, they have been lucky enough to experience many new cultures and customs together.

The couple took a major step in early 2024, purchasing their first home together a few miles east in La Mesa. Through the challenges of first-time homeownership (including a new roof, central A/C install, and countless other repairs…), Clay and Dan have further strengthened their partnership and commitment to each other.

In typical fashion, a new backyard meant that a new puppy was soon to follow. The couple adopted their dachshund, Poppy, in December 2024 and she has quickly become the queen of the house. She enjoys accompanying Dan and Clay on trips to local parks, beaches, and breweries. While she won't be at the wedding, she sends her love and kisses to you all.

A lifelong commitment

The proposal

While on vacation in France, Clay finally proposed to Dan on September 3rd, 2025. Following a day at the Louvre and unbeknownst to Dan, Clay had chartered a private sunset cruise on the Seine. While walking along the Left Bank, Dan was surprised to be flagged down by a captain at a small dock who greeted the couple with rosé and macarons. Clay and Dan enjoyed the cruise around the river, taking in the sights as the sun set over Paris.

After they disembarked, Clay and Dan walked further along the Seine and found a place to sit directly beneath the Eiffel Tower near the Pont d'Iéna. As the Eiffel Tower began to sparkle, Clay asked Dan to quickly turn and take a photo of the scene. As Dan turned back around, he found Clay on one knee (dangerously close to the river) with a ring in hand. Dan said "Yes!" through joyful tears and they both have been looking forward to the wedding ever since.
